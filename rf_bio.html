QD Bio

Builder. Writer. Sovereignty enthusiast. Occasional over-caffeinated optimist.

Hi. I’m Juan Jose.
I build worlds, then I build systems that work in the real one.

Bio, translated into plain English:
I got tired of “renting” the future… so I started forging it.

The short version

I’m a creative at heart and a builder by default. In the last year and a half, I wrote over 20 full novels, because apparently my brain doesn’t believe in weekends, or the concept of “just one more chapter.”

And here’s the part most people don’t realize: writing at that pace isn’t “inspiration.” It’s systems. It’s discipline. It’s learning how to show up when the vibe is gone, keep continuity across massive story arcs, and still land the ending like you meant it. When you can ship that many complete worlds, you learn how to execute under pressure, hit deadlines, and keep quality high without losing your mind (or at least without losing it permanently).

That speed also forces a kind of ruthless clarity: what matters, what doesn’t, what’s fluff, and what actually moves the story forward. And it turns out that mindset transfers perfectly into building companies: ship the real thing, remove the noise, and keep the mission clean.

That pace taught me something important: if you can ship complete worlds consistently, you can ship real products too, as long as you stay honest, stay organized, and keep the mission clean.

Rarefolio.io is my FT & NFT creation company

One of my proudest creative builds is that I rewrote the Jules Verne classic Michael Strogoff, or The Courier of the Czar. I didn’t just “modernize a few lines”, I rebuilt it for modern readers while keeping the spirit intact. Then I created downloadable cover and chapter art to make the experience feel like a collector-grade package, not just another text dump on a screen.

And because I apparently enjoy ambitious projects the way other people enjoy relaxing, I took it further: I translated the entire two-book series into modern English from the older form, and I just finished translating that modern English version into Spanish. The goal is to have it live on KDP by February 2026, clean, readable, and built for real readers in both languages.

That’s the creative engine behind Rarefolio. I’m tuning my storytelling obsession and builder mindset into something practical and powerful: sovereign-based business software for real business owners who want to run their world without feeling like they’re renting their own operations from someone else.

Translation: fewer dashboards that need therapy… more systems that just work.

That means:

  • A POS that doesn’t treat you like a guest in your own store.
  • A rewards system that starts as points and evolves into crypto-based rewards.
  • Built for any size business, without turning the owner into an unpaid IT intern.

Then I added silver. Because… of course I did.

Most digital collectibles feel like “a pretty file that lives somewhere.” I wanted something that feels more like an artifact something you’re proud to display and happy to own.

So I fused three things:

  1. Beautiful, display-worthy CNFT artwork (gallery vibes, real interior value).
  2. Provenance-first structure (clean IDs, clean series logic, collector confidence).
  3. A real-world anchor: the silver bar mindset.

The result is simple: a CNFT that adds beauty and wealth to any place it’s displayed, home office, hotel lobby, lounge, studio, boardroom… wherever you want your walls to quietly say: “I collect with intention.”

Think: a private vault meets a gallery, but without your laptop’s GPU filing a complaint.

How We Operate

Collector-first design

We optimize for fast browsing, strong mobile viewing, clean navigation, and a premium “catalog of the future” experience.

Discipline in the details

We prefer consistency over cleverness, structure over chaos, and systems that scale cleanly, without mutating into “inventory gremlins” as volume grows. Every process is deliberate, repeatable, and designed to remain intelligible at 40 Collectible Originals or 40,000. Nothing here is accidental, improvised, or left to chance.

Each image in our collections is personally hand-selected, not bulk-generated, not algorithmically padded, and never filler. What the future Art Owner receives is not merely a digital token, but a curated, exhibition-grade collectible original, prepared with real-world use in mind.

To reinforce long-term value through disciplined scarcity and clear provenance, each CNFT in the offering is paired conceptually with a Silver Bar Token as part of a 40,000-token series that corresponds one-to-one with the 40,000 CNFTs in the collection. The token’s metadata is explicit: the CNFT is not redeemable for physical silver. It does not represent a claim, entitlement, or right to metal. Instead, it exists to lend contextual and narrative value to the CNFT, anchoring the art inside a coherent, serial-minded framework built for scale, clarity, and trust.

Human curation & quality control

While advanced tools may assist parts of the creative workflow, final image selection, validation, and production quality control are always performed by humans. This responsibility is never delegated to automation.

Every image that enters a Rarefolio collection is reviewed by a human eye, approved by intent, and validated against a defined production checklist. Nothing is “auto-passed.” Nothing is bulk-approved. Nothing ships without accountability.

Each approved image is manually evaluated for visual integrity, composition, and suitability for the collection, then routed to the correct master PSD workflow, prepared to exact specifications, and placed into its correct, versioned folder structure before final output.

This process exists for one reason: systems earn trust when humans remain responsible for outcomes.

AI is a tool. It can assist. It can accelerate. It can inspire. But it does not curate, it does not approve, and it does not ship.

We believe collectors deserve to know that a real person stood behind every collectible original, checked it, approved it, and ensured it met the same standards as every other piece in the collection. This is how we maintain consistency, prevent drift, and protect long-term quality as the system scales.

Automation may support the workflow. Human judgment remains the gatekeeper.

Sovereignty is the point

Rarefolio is building more than collectibles, we are building software systems that foster real sovereignty for everyone involved. That means clarity of ownership, transparent structure, verifiable provenance, and tools that reduce dependency on intermediaries where it matters. Sovereignty is not a slogan here; it is a design constraint. If a feature increases confusion, adds friction, or weakens the collector’s confidence, it does not ship.

Practical value, not just aesthetics

Our work is meant to be used, displayed, printed, showcased, by collectors and institutions that want visual impact with real ownership logic behind it.

Why this matters to me

This isn’t just “a project.” It’s a philosophy of life in product form: sovereignty, clarity, and ownership, expressed through software, art, and disciplined systems.

If something adds confusion, adds friction, or makes people feel like they’re renting their own value… I’m not interested. Rarefolio is built for the opposite: simple ownership logic and a collector experience that respects the user.
