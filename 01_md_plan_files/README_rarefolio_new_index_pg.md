# rarefolio — Internal Project Readme for deciding which new index page we will have


Version 1: The Vault Entrance

This is the strongest flagship option.

# //// index page idea #1 The Vault Entrance \\\\\\\\\\  
Think of the homepage as entering a private exhibition vault. The first screen is full-screen, cinematic, dark, metallic, controlled, with one unforgettable statement such as:

Rarefolio.io
Museum-Grade CNFT Collections Mapped to Physical Silver Provenance

Below that, only two primary actions:
Enter the Collection
View Founders Prelaunch

Under the hero, instead of dumping multiple collection grids, you create a sequence of high-status sections:

1. The Rarefolio Thesis
A short, sharp explanation of what makes this different: tokenized silver bars, serial-mapped provenance, collector-grade presentation, Cardano infrastructure, limited blocks, long-term archive value.

2. Featured Exhibition
One featured collection only. Not Taurus and Aries and Inventors all at once. One spotlight. Big image. Curatorial intro. “Now Exhibiting.”

3. The Three Pillars
Physical provenance. Museum-quality digital art. Long-horizon collector architecture.

4. Collector Pathways
Three elegant cards:

Explore Collections
Verify Provenance
Join Founders Prelaunch

5. Archive Preview
A refined strip of 6 pieces, not giant stacked grids.

6. Trust + Standards
Serial logic, release structure, collection integrity, future secondary market vision.

Why this works:
It makes Rarefolio feel expensive, intentional, and sovereign. It also follows best practice by giving users a clear homepage purpose, strong navigation pathways, and a restrained hierarchy instead of content overload.

Best for:
Your “museum-quality flagship” goal.

Version 2: The Digital Museum

This one leans harder into art, curation, and cultural positioning.

# //// index page idea #2 The Digital Museum \\\\\\\\\\  
The homepage opens like a museum exhibition landing page rather than a marketplace. The tone is more refined, editorial, and prestigious. Less “browse cards,” more “curated narrative.”

Structure:

Hero:
A dramatic exhibition banner with rotating collection art and a line like:
Collected as Art. Structured as Provenance. Preserved on Cardano.

Section 1: Current Exhibition
One collection presented as a museum show with title, curator-style intro, and “View Exhibition.”

Section 2: The Permanent Collection
A beautifully designed section introducing the broader Rarefolio universe: Zodiac, Inventors Guild, Dreamscape canon, future bars.

Section 3: Collection Rooms
Instead of card grids, each collection gets a horizontal “gallery room” panel with one master image, one sentence, one CTA.

Section 4: Why Rarefolio Exists
This is where your philosophy hits: beauty, permanence, ownership, digital provenance, and serious collecting.

Section 5: Acquisition Path
A luxury version of onboarding:

Discover
Study
Collect
Hold
Trade on secondary market

Section 6: Founder / Artist / Collector Invitation
A prestige callout for serious participants.

Why this works:
Museum sites are most effective when they act as a digital gateway to the collection, not a cluttered pile of thumbnails. This route makes Rarefolio feel culturally important rather than merely available for sale.
